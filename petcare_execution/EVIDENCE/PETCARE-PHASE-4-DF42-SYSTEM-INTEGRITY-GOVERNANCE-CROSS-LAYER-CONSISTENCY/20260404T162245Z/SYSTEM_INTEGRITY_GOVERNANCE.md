DF42 — System Integrity Governance + Cross-Layer Consistency

Purpose

Establish the cross-layer integrity constitution for PetCare so that trust governance, network governance, economic governance, commercial fairness governance, and dispute accountability governance remain mutually consistent, non-conflicting, loophole-resistant, and fail-closed when combined.

Authority Boundary

This layer governs cross-layer consistency. It does not authorize autonomous conflict resolution, autonomous policy override, autonomous exception approval, or silent precedence reassignment.

State Transition

Prior state:
TRUST_GOVERNED + NETWORK_EFFECTS_GOVERNED + ECONOMICALLY_GOVERNED + COMMERCIAL_FAIRNESS_GOVERNED + DISPUTE_ACCOUNTABILITY_GOVERNED

DF42 target state:
TRUST_GOVERNED + NETWORK_EFFECTS_GOVERNED + ECONOMICALLY_GOVERNED + COMMERCIAL_FAIRNESS_GOVERNED + DISPUTE_ACCOUNTABILITY_GOVERNED + SYSTEM_INTEGRITY_GOVERNED

Core Principles

1. No two governance layers may create contradictory permitted outcomes
2. Lower layers may not bypass higher-sensitivity controls through combination effects
3. Cross-layer precedence must be explicit, reviewable, and fail-closed
4. Every exception path must be linked to a declared governance owner and approval reference
5. Clinical safety, trust, and fairness controls may not be weakened by commercial or dispute logic
6. Ambiguity across layers must block activation until resolved
7. Cross-layer integrity evidence must be auditable and reproducible
8. AI may assist with integrity analysis only; AI may not decide precedence or approve overrides

Cross-Layer Integrity Domains

Trust to Economic Consistency
Ensures incentives, pricing, and value distribution do not violate trust or tier principles

Economic to Fairness Consistency
Ensures incentives and revenue structures do not create hidden or unjustified treatment advantage

Fairness to Dispute Consistency
Ensures dispute handling does not contradict fairness protections or enable retaliatory exceptions

Network to Commercial Consistency
Ensures amplification, visibility, and behavioral loops do not covertly distort commercial opportunity

System Precedence Governance
Defines which control wins when multiple governance layers apply simultaneously

Control Modes

Mode 1
BLOCKED
Required cross-layer references or integrity approvals are missing

Mode 2
CONTROLLED
Cross-layer integrity model exists but enforcement remains inactive

Mode 3
ACTIVE_GOVERNED
Cross-layer consistency controls are active, reviewed, and evidenced

Fail-Closed Conditions

The system must block activation when any of the following are missing:
1. named integrity owner
2. approved integrity governance approval reference
3. approved cross-layer precedence ruleset reference
4. approved conflict resolution standard reference
5. declared integrity review mode

Mandatory Outputs

1. integrity decision log
2. cross-layer consistency reference
3. conflict catalog reference
4. prohibited loophole pattern reference
5. precedence model reference
6. rollback posture
7. manifest with SHA-256

Non-Negotiable Invariants

1. no autonomous policy override
2. no silent precedence drift
3. no loophole chaining across layers
4. no hidden exception paths
5. no weakening of higher-sensitivity controls
6. no unresolved ambiguity during activation
7. no policy drift from DF37 to DF41
8. commit remains the single source of truth
