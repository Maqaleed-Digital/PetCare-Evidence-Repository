DF41 Dispute Intake and Resolution Policy

Policy Objective

Ensure disputes, exceptions, complaints, and contested actions are handled through formal, reviewable, and accountable governance rather than ad hoc or retaliatory treatment.

Policy Rules

DR-01
Every dispute must have an owner, approval reference, classification ruleset reference, remediation standard reference, and declared review mode.

DR-02
Every dispute must declare its class:
1. pricing dispute
2. revenue-share dispute
3. incentive dispute
4. fairness challenge
5. routing or visibility challenge
6. partner treatment complaint
7. remediation non-compliance
8. appeal request

DR-03
Every dispute must identify the contested action, timeframe, affected party, and linked evidence references.

DR-04
No dispute may be closed without resolution rationale, accountable reviewer identity, and closure status.

DR-05
Any dispute that materially affects opportunity, revenue access, partner standing, or commercial treatment requires human review before resolution.

DR-06
Any remediation arising from dispute review must include owner, due date, evidence update requirement, and rollback or restoration posture where applicable.

DR-07
No dispute process may directly or indirectly alter clinical safety decisions, emergency escalation, medication handling, or consent enforcement for commercial purposes.

DR-08
Retaliation, informal suppression, silent de-prioritization, or undeclared commercial disadvantage against a disputing party is prohibited.

DR-09
Appeal must remain available for in-scope dispute classes and must preserve the full evidence chain from the original decision.

Minimum Evidence per Dispute

1. dispute_id
2. owner
3. approval reference
4. dispute class
5. contested action reference
6. affected party or cohort
7. review mode
8. reason summary
9. valid review window
10. remediation requirement if any
11. appeal path
12. audit trace id
