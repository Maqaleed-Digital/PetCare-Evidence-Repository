PHASE 4 EXECUTION SEQUENCE PLAN

PURPOSE

Define the sequenced execution structure for the whole of Phase 4.

PHASE 4 SEQUENCE

DF37 — TRUST FRAMEWORK + PARTNER TIER GOVERNANCE
Role:
- define trust framework, partner tiering, evidence-backed trust posture rules, and tier governance constraints

DF38 — NETWORK EFFECT GOVERNANCE + VALUE LOOP CONTROL
Role:
- define governed network effects, partner value loops, participation reinforcement controls, and no-coercion boundaries

DF39 — MARKET INCENTIVE GOVERNANCE + ECONOMIC CONTROL LAYER
Role:
- define pricing influence constraints, revenue-share governance posture, incentive approval rules, and economic auditability

DF40 — ECOSYSTEM QUALITY SIGNALS + MARKET REPUTATION CONTROLS
Role:
- define quality signals, reputation evidence model, no-uncited scoring rule, and quality-driven ecosystem preference governance

DF41 — REGULATORY EXPOSURE READINESS + EXTERNAL TRUST ASSURANCE
Role:
- define market-facing compliance exposure readiness, third-party trust assurance posture, and regulator-grade evidence alignment

DF42 — DOMINANCE SUSTAINABILITY + ANTI-DRIFT CONTROL
Role:
- define how market leadership remains governed, reversible, non-coercive, and protected from trust drift or governance dilution

STAGE LOGIC

Stage 1
- trust and participation structure

Stage 2
- network and economic governance

Stage 3
- quality, assurance, and dominance sustainability

FAIL-CLOSED RULE

If any prior workstream is incomplete, stale, misaligned, or lacks evidence:
- downstream workstream is blocked

PHASE 4 COMPLETION CONDITION

Phase 4 completes only when:
- trusted ecosystem scale is governed
- market-facing influence is controlled
- quality and reputation remain evidence-backed
- regulatory exposure is readiness-aligned
- dominance mechanics remain reversible, auditable, and non-coercive
