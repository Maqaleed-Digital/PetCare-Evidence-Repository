PHASE 4 SCOPE AND CONSTRAINT MAP

PURPOSE

Define the scope boundaries for the whole Phase 4 program.

IN SCOPE

- trust framework governance
- partner tier governance
- network effect governance
- ecosystem value loop control
- economic and incentive governance
- quality signal governance
- reputation evidence governance
- regulatory exposure readiness
- external trust assurance posture
- dominance sustainability and anti-drift control

OUT OF SCOPE

- autonomous market optimization
- uncited trust or reputation scoring
- coercive partner lock-in mechanics
- uncontrolled economic incentives
- policy exception creation
- irreversible market dominance mechanisms
- infra, IAM, or DNS mutation through this pack

PHASE 4 HARD BOUNDARIES

- trust must remain evidence-backed
- tiering must remain explainable and approval-bound
- network effects must remain governed and non-coercive
- economic controls must remain auditable
- reputation must never be uncited
- assurance readiness must remain regulator-defensible
- dominance must remain reversible, non-coercive, and anti-drift governed
- local, federated, portfolio, ecosystem, and activation controls remain cumulative

PHASE 4 SUCCESS CRITERIA

- every workstream is staged and traceable
- every trust and dominance posture is evidence-backed
- every quality and reputation signal is governed
- every market-facing mechanism is reversible
- ecosystem scale strengthens control rather than weakening it
