DF42 Cross-Layer Precedence and Integrity Policy

Policy Objective

Ensure all governance layers operate as one coherent system with explicit precedence, conflict handling, and fail-closed behavior.

Policy Rules

SI-01
Every cross-layer activation must have an integrity owner, approval reference, precedence ruleset reference, conflict resolution standard reference, and review mode.

SI-02
Every cross-layer review must identify the involved governance layers:
1. trust
2. network effects
3. economic control
4. commercial fairness
5. dispute accountability

SI-03
Where multiple layers apply, the higher-sensitivity control prevails unless an explicitly approved exception path exists.

SI-04
Clinical safety, trust protection, fairness protection, and dispute appeal rights may not be weakened by commercial or economic convenience.

SI-05
Any ambiguity in precedence, exception scope, or combined outcome blocks activation until resolved.

SI-06
Every cross-layer exception must include owner, rationale, expiry, review window, and rollback path.

SI-07
No cross-layer combination may create a result that no single layer would independently permit.

SI-08
Every integrity review must preserve evidence of the compared rules, detected conflict, chosen precedence, and final outcome.

Minimum Evidence per Integrity Action

1. integrity_action_id
2. owner
3. approval reference
4. involved layers
5. precedence decision
6. conflict summary
7. review mode
8. valid review window
9. rollback rule
10. audit trace id
