PETCARE-STEADY-STATE-OPERATIONS-AND-CONTINUOUS-IMPROVEMENT-GOVERNANCE
STEADY_STATE_OPERATING_MODEL

Purpose
Define the business-as-usual operating model after hypercare closeout.

State Transition
- Current: petcare_hypercare_governed_operations_active
- Target: petcare_steady_state_governed_operations_active

Operating Model
- named service owner
- named operations owner
- named technical owner
- named governance owner
- weekly service review cadence
- monthly executive and board review cadence
- formal change calendar
- problem management ownership
- vendor and partner review cadence
- continuous improvement backlog ownership

Minimum BAU Coverage
- runtime health review
- incident and problem review
- KPI and SLO review
- audit and AI governance continuity review
- release calendar and change review
- clinic and partner service review
- open improvement backlog review

Rules
- ownership must remain named
- review cadence must remain active
- critical governance paths must remain in routine review
- unowned Sev-1 and Sev-2 issues are not permitted in steady-state operations

Success Rule
Steady-state operations are active only when ownership, cadence, and review controls are documented and linked to evidence.
